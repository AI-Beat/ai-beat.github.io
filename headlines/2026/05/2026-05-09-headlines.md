# AI Headlines — 2026-05-09

- [Rethinking RL for LLM Reasoning: Policy Selection, Not Capability Expansion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06241) — Challenges the narrative that RL teaches reasoning models new skills; evidence shows only 1–3% of token positions are affected, the promoted token always lies within the base model's existing top-5 alternatives, and base-model entropy alone identifies these positions without any RL-trained signal. *(May 8, 2026)*

- [Can LLMs Model Real-World Systems in TLA+?](https://www.sigops.org/2026/can-llms-model-real-world-systems-in-tla/) — Introduces SysMoBench, a four-phase benchmark evaluating LLM-generated formal specs of distributed systems: models score near-perfect on syntax but only ~46% on conformance and ~41% on invariant checking, with the core failure being that LLMs reproduce what's in papers rather than what implementations actually do. *(May 8, 2026)*

- [Teaching Claude Why](https://www.anthropic.com/research/teaching-claude-why) — Anthropic's alignment research finds that training on examples where Claude displays admirable *reasoning* for aligned behavior generalizes better than training on demonstrations of correct actions; out-of-distribution constitutional documents and fictional narratives outperformed direct eval-targeted data. *(May 8, 2026)*

- [Cited but Not Verified: Source Attribution in Deep Research Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06635) — Evaluates citation quality across frontier deep-research agents; URL validity exceeds 94% and relevance 80%, but factual accuracy falls to 39–77%; accuracy drops ~42% as tool calls scale from 2 to 150, showing that more retrieval does not produce more accurate citations. *(May 8, 2026)*

- [Mojo 1.0 Beta](https://mojolang.org/) — Modular releases the first beta of Mojo, the Python-compatible compiled language targeting AI hardware (CPU and GPU); supports direct GPU kernel development without vendor-specific libraries and Python-native interoperability. *(May 7, 2026)*
