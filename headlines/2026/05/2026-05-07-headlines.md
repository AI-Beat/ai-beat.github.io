# AI Headlines — 2026-05-07

- [ProgramBench: Can Language Models Rebuild Programs from Scratch?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03546) — Meta, Stanford, and Harvard benchmark 9 LLMs on reconstructing real programs (FFmpeg, SQLite, PHP) from only a binary and docs; no model fully resolves any task, and the best (Claude Opus 4.7) passes 95%+ of tests on only 3% of tasks. *(May 5, 2026)*

- [Vibe coding and agentic engineering are getting closer than I'd like](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/6/vibe-coding-and-agentic-engineering/) — Simon Willison observes that the boundary between careless AI code acceptance and disciplined agentic engineering is eroding even for experienced practitioners: he no longer reviews every line of production AI-generated code, and rationalizes it with a team-trust mental model. *(May 6, 2026)*

- [Learning the Integral of a Diffusion Model](https://sander.ai/2026/05/06/flow-maps.html) — Sander Dieleman (Google DeepMind) proposes flow maps—neural nets that predict any point along a diffusion path from any other point, enabling single-step sampling—and shows how this integral formulation unifies 20+ recent papers on diffusion distillation under three consistency constraints. *(May 6, 2026)*

- [Show HN: Tilde.run – Agent sandbox with a transactional, versioned filesystem](https://tilde.run/) — Open platform that wraps AI agent runs as atomic transactions over a unified filesystem (GitHub, S3, Google Drive); changes stage until success and commit atomically, or roll back on failure, with per-agent permissions and network audit trails. *(May 7, 2026)*
