# AI Headlines — 2026-07-16

- [Inkling: Our Open-Weights Model](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/) — Thinking Machines Lab (Mira Murati's startup) releases its first public model: a 975B total / 41B active MoE trained on 45T multimodal tokens, Apache 2.0, with strong reasoning benchmarks (AIME 2026 97.1%, SWEBench 77.6%) and a deliberate "not the strongest, but the most customizable" pitch. *(July 15, 2026)*

- [Grok Build is open source](https://github.com/xai-org/grok-build) — Two days after a wire-level analysis showed the Grok Build CLI uploads full git bundles (including .env files) to Google Cloud Storage, xAI open-sourced the tool under Apache 2.0; the single-commit repository makes the upload behavior auditable for the first time. *(July 16, 2026)*

- [Can LLMs Perform Deep Technical Comprehension of Computer Architecture Papers?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11859) — Gauntlet, an open-source pipeline that runs five independent expert-persona reviewers and an adversarial synthesis stage on a paper, was preferred over human expert analysis on 15 of 20 ISCA 2025 and HPCA 2026 papers — raising the question of what "deep" technical reading actually means. *(July 13, 2026)*
