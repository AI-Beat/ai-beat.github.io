# AI Headlines — 2026-07-14

- [Jacquard: a programming language for AI-written, human-reviewed code](https://github.com/jbwinters/jacquard-lang) — Research prototype PL that models effects, discrete uncertainty, and content-addressed identity directly in the language syntax, so human reviewers can see the blast radius and trust surface of AI-generated code without reading every line. *(July 14, 2026)*

- [Apple's SpeechAnalyzer API benchmarked against Whisper](https://get-inscribe.com/blog/apple-speech-api-benchmark.html) — Inscribe benchmarks Apple's new on-device SpeechAnalyzer (macOS 26.5.1) against Whisper Small/Base/Tiny on 5,559 LibriSpeech utterances; SpeechAnalyzer achieves 2.12% WER vs Whisper Small's 3.74% while running ~3× faster, though it covers only ~30 languages vs Whisper's 100+. *(July 13, 2026)*

- [A Study of Microsoft's Early 2026 Rollout of Claude Code and GitHub Copilot CLI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01418) — Analysis of tens of thousands of engineers over 4 months finds adopters merged ~24% more PRs than they would have otherwise; adoption spread primarily through social networks rather than top-down mandate. *(July 1, 2026)*

- [Recursive Self-Improvement in AI: From Bounded Self-Refinement to Autonomous Research Loops](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07663) — Survey of 1,250 arXiv papers (2024–2026) organizes RSI work along two axes (what gets improved × degree of loop closure), identifies measurement and evaluation as the most neglected research area, and flags self-confirming loops as the critical failure mode. *(July 8, 2026)*
