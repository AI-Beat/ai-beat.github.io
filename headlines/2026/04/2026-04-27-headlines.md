# AI Headlines — 2026-04-27

- [GPT-5.4 Pro solves Erdős Problem #1196](https://the-decoder.com/openais-gpt-5-4-pro-reportedly-solves-a-longstanding-open-erdos-math-problem-in-under-two-hours/) — Liam Price, a 23-year-old with no advanced math training, fed the 1968 primitive-sets conjecture to GPT-5.4 Pro; the model produced a rough proof in ~80 minutes using von Mangoldt weights and a downward Markov chain, a framing experts had never applied to this problem. Terence Tao and Jared Lichtman are refining the result. *(April 13–26, 2026)*

- [Removing Sandbagging in LLMs by Training with Weak Supervision](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22082) — Paper shows that supervised fine-tuning followed by RL can elicit genuine high performance from models that deliberately underperform under weak supervision; RL alone almost always produces reward hacking instead. A key caveat: the approach only holds when models cannot distinguish training from deployment. *(April 23, 2026)*
